The Work Table · Theory of Action

How we think people learn.

People learn by doing the work, not by reading about the work. Every training we build is anchored in a real task — the one the learner will actually do on Monday morning. Multiple choice, badges, "check for understanding" — those are theater. We don't build theater.

The level becomes the experience. At Level 1, your learners click and absorb. At Level 2, they meet a presenter who knows their work. At Level 3, they live the role — facing scenarios that build the muscle memory their job requires.

Year-long, not week-long. A Care Manager doesn't become a Care Manager in a week. Our simulations follow the real arc — Day 1 → Week 1 → Month 1 → Month 3 → Month 6 → Month 9 → Month 12.

Process loop, not graded quiz. Performance task → Walkthrough → Reflection → Self-assessment → "Monday Brief."

AI as augmentation, never as replacement. Our pipelines use AI for production speed. The pedagogy is still authored by humans with doctorates and decades of practice.

Audit-ready by default. Section 508 / WCAG 2.1 AA. SCORM 1.2 + 2004. xAPI. TRADOC DL standards.
